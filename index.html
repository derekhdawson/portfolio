Work Experience

June 2017 - September 2017

NOMAD is a startup that aims to create a new medium in advertising called Human-Powered Advertising. It works by providing highly visible people, called Nomad’s, that carry iPads on their backpacks on college campuses and events. The iPads display ads that companies supply through their customer dashboard.

I was hired as a freelance programmer to build the MVP for both the website and iPad in 4 months. After launch, we continued to grow signing Zipcar, Limebike, Soylent and now have presence around the country at over 10 Universities.

June 2017 - September 2017

I started on the SEO team as a frontend developer. I learned important technologies related to SEO, like AB testing, internal linking systems, caching pages, improving page load speed, and SEO tools like Google Analytics. I was excited to be exposed to such modern and well thought out frontend applications as one my goals coming in was to understand how complex web apps are organized. A month in I built a completely new page which helped cement the concepts that I had learned thus far.

Halfway through my internship I switched to the pro team. Luckily, I joined the team while they were working on incorporating phone leads, a new major feature. This feature didn’t require much frontend work, so for the rest of my internship I did backend using Java and Scala. One major technology that this feature was built with was Kafka. We used Kafka for its publish and subscribe capabilities to respond to events in real-time. My task was to ensure Pros' company document were always up to date in order to ensure phone call eligibility. Through this task I learned about the importance of decoupling through Kafka, serializing objects, functional programming with Scala, and learned to perform SQL migrations through Flyway. I was also exposed to how the microservices architecture works and why we chose to use Kubernetes and Dropwizard to serve our purposes.

I feel fortunate to have had such a great experience and be able to work with multiple teams. As a curious individual it was satisfying to dive deeply in to both frontend and backend because it allowed me to see the whole picture of our product and understand how the stack really worked from end to end.
